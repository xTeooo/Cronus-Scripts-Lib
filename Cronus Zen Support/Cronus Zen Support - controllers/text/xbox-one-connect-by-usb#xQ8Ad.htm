Connect by USB

Connect your favorite supported Xbox, PlayStation, or Nintendo controllers to a Cronus Zen (w/ USB Cable) and an Xbox One.

ğŸ§ HEADSET AUDIO

While Zen supports most wired headsets connecting to the controller, you should know that audio is heavy on the CPU. This isn't a problem if you do not use GamePacks or scripts, but if you are, audio may affect its performance by causing the controller to disconnect or introduce lag. It is highly recommended that if you are using powerful MODs, switch to a wireless headset or any headset that connects directly to the console and not the controller.

ğŸ® XBOX (USB)

â›” WARNING

Original Microsoft Xbox Controllers updated to 5.15.3165.0 are disconnecting after a few minutes. Currently this is an Xbox Insiders Program firmware so is not available to the public yet, so if you have updated you should be able to revert to 5.13.3146.0.

Setup Hardware: Xbox One console, XBOX SERIES X/S, XBOX ELITE S2, and XBOX ONE controllers, Zen Studio and Zen Firmware .

ï»¿IMPORTANT: Make sure your Xbox controller is fully charged/fresh batteries, or it will likely disconnect or not detect at all.

  1. Connect CRONUS ZEN to PC from PROG (mini-USB).
  2. Open ZEN STUDIO > Device Panel > Emulator Output Protocol set to AUTO.
  3. Make sure that PS4 Speciality and PS Remote Play are NOT enabled.
  4. Unplug all USB cables from Zen (no power).
  5. Connect CRONUS ZEN to the XBOX ONE CONSOLE from CONSOLE/PC (micro-USB).
  6. Connect the XBOX controller to A1 and press the XBOX button to power it on.
If successful, the Zen RGBs will change to solid GREEN, and the XBOX LED will stop flashing and change to solid WHITE, indicating that Zen, controller, and console are now correctly paired. If not? make sure the controller is powered on by pressing the XBOX button, then press Zen's reset button a couple of times and that should re-sync everything.

ï»¿AUDIO: You can connect a wired headset to most XBOX controllers. However, it is recommended that you use a wireless headset, or one that connects directly to the console and not the controller. For more details see the Wired Headset Users page.ï»¿ï»¿

Setup Hardware: Xbox One console, DUALSENSE, and DUALSHOCK 4 v2 controllers, Zen Studio and Zen Firmware .

ï»¿IMPORTANT: Make sure your PlayStation controller is fully charged, or it will likely disconnect or not detect at all.

  1. Connect the CRONUS ZEN to a PC from PROG (mini-USB).
  2. Open ZEN STUDIO > Device Panel > Emulator Output Protocol set to AUTO.
  3. Make sure that PS4 Speciality and PS Remote Play are NOT enabled. NOTE: Unplug all USB cables from Zen (no power).
  4. Connect the CRONUS ZEN to an XBOX ONE CONSOLE from CONSOLE/PC (micro-USB).
  5. Connect an XBOX ONE controller to A1(license donor).
  6. Connect the PLAYSTATION controller to A3 and press both XBOX and PS buttons to power them on.
If successful, the Zen RGBs and DUALSHOCK 4 lightbar will change to solid GREENï»¿ï»¿, and the DUALSENSE touchpad changes to BLUEï»¿ï»¿, indicating that Zen, controller, and console are now correctly paired. If not? make sure the controllers are both powered on by pressing the PS and XBOX buttons, then press Zen's reset button a couple of times and that should re-sync everything.

ï»¿AUDIO: You can connect a wired headset to the PlayStation controller, or if that doesn't work try the A1 Xbox donor controller, as that may work. However, it is recommended that you use a wireless headset or one that connects directly to the console and not the controller. For more details see the Wired Headset Users page.

ğŸš¦ LIVE MONITORING

If you wish to monitor controller outputs in real-time with Zen Studio, connect the PROG (mini-USB) cable from Zen to PC, and keep the Zen connected to your Xbox One with the CONSOLE/PC (micro-USB) cable. Open Zen Studio and make sure the Zen is detected, then go to the Device panel and select Tools > Device Monitor (Shortcut: F3).

â˜¢ï¸ TROUBLESHOOTING

If the Zen RGB LEDs do not light up, or if no controller buttons are responding, unplug all USB cables from the Zen, reconnect, then press Zen's reset button and that should fix it. Make sure the controllers are powered on.

If your controller disconnects:

  • The controller connected to A1 does not work as a donor (e.g., DS4 v1).
  • The USB cable from the controller to the Zen is bad.
  • The USB port on the controller is damaged.
