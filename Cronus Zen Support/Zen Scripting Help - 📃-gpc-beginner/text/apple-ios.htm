Setup Hardware: Apple iPAD (8th Gen iOS 15.4.1), Lightning Camera Adapter, DUALSHOCK 4 v2 controller, Zen Studio , and Zen Firmware .

  1. Connect the CRONUS ZEN to a PC from PROG (mini-USB).
  2. Open ZEN STUDIO > Device Panel > Select "Enable PS Remote Play"
  3. Wait for the Zen to restart, then check PS Remote Play is enabled (unplug and reconnect all USB cables from the Zen if it doesn't detect in Zen Studio after the restart).
  4. Unplug all USB cables from Zen (no power).
  5. Connect the CRONUS ZEN to an APPLE iOS DEVICE from CONSOLE/PC (micro-USB + USB-C OTG adapter). NOTE: If your Apple device has a lightning port instead of USB-C, you can use an Apple Lightning Camera Adapter.
  6. Connect a DUALSHOCK 4 controller to A3 and press the PS button to power it on.
If successful, the Zen RGBs will change to WHITE and the DUALSHOCK 4 lightbar will change to solid BLUEï»¿ï»¿, indicating that Zen, controller, and Apple device are now correctly paired. If not? make sure the controller is powered on by pressing the PS button, then press Zen's reset button a couple of times and that should re-sync everything.

TOUCHSCREEN: You can use the touchscreen at the same time as using a controller.

If you wish to monitor controller outputs in real-time with Zen Studio, connect the PROG (mini-USB) cable from Zen to PC, and keep the Zen connected to your Apple device with the CONSOLE/PC (micro-USB + USB-C OTG adapter or Lightning Camera Adapter) cable. Open Zen Studio and make sure the Zen is detected, then go to the Device panel and select Tools > Device Monitor (Shortcut: F3).

If the Zen RGB LEDs do not light up, or if no controller buttons are responding, unplug all USB cables from the Zen, reconnect, then press Zen's reset button and that should fix it. Make sure the controllers are powered on.

If your controller disconnects:

  • If the controller connected to A3 does not work, try A1.
  • The USB cable from the controller to the Zen is bad.
  • The USB port on the controller is damaged.
  • The adapter to the Apple device is not connected or a bad connection (Lightning Camera Adapter or USB-C OTG Adapter). You should clean the Lightning Port/USB-C port as these can cause disconnections or no connection at all.
  • The Apple Device has an M1 chipset which may not work.
